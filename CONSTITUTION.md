# Constitution of the Executive Intelligence Briefing (EIB)

> Every solution should leave behind a better starting point for the next person.

---

## Preamble

The Executive Intelligence Briefing (EIB) exists to preserve knowledge, improve decision-making, and transform experience into reusable intelligence.

This Constitution defines the enduring principles that govern the EIB project. These principles are intended to be stable over time and should guide every architectural decision, contribution, and enhancement.

When conflicts arise between convenience and principle, this Constitution takes precedence.

---

# Article I — Purpose

The purpose of EIB is to capture knowledge in a way that enables future contributors to accomplish more with less effort.

Knowledge should become easier to discover, understand, and reuse over time.

---

# Article II — Stewardship

Contributors are stewards of the repository, not owners of it.

Every contribution should improve the repository for those who follow.

---

# Article III — Single Source of Truth

Each concept should have one authoritative home.

Duplication should be avoided whenever practical.

Templates should be generalized.

Implementations should reference templates rather than replace them.

---

# Article IV — Public Before Private

Knowledge that can benefit others belongs in the public repository.

Personal implementations belong in private repositories.

When both are appropriate, publish the reusable framework publicly and maintain the implementation privately.

---

# Article V — Evolution

EIB evolves continuously.

Existing work should be improved before it is replaced.

Architectural decisions should be documented through Architecture Decision Records (ADRs).

---

# Article VI — Simplicity

Favor clarity over cleverness.

Favor maintainability over novelty.

Favor reusable systems over one-time solutions.

---

# Article VII — Governance

Meaningful architectural changes should be reviewed before implementation.

Major decisions should be documented.

Repository organization should reflect long-term maintainability.

---

# Article VIII — Quality

Every contribution should leave the repository in a better state than it was found.

Every solution should reduce future effort.

Every document should become easier to understand.

---

# Article IX — Future Contributors

EIB is optimized for future contributors rather than current convenience.

Decisions should consider not only today's needs, but how they will serve contributors years from now.

---

## Closing Statement

The Executive Intelligence Briefing is more than a collection of documents.

It is a commitment to preserving knowledge, sharing experience, and continuously improving the foundation upon which others will build.
