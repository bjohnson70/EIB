# Executive Intelligence Briefing Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

## Purpose

This document defines the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the Executive Intelligence Briefing (EIB) platform.

The MVP represents the smallest implementation that delivers meaningful value to an executive while validating the platform architecture and establishing a foundation for future growth.

---

# Philosophy

An MVP is not a simplified vision.

It is the smallest complete solution that solves a real problem.

The objective is to begin generating daily executive value as early as possible while minimizing implementation complexity.

---

# Objectives

The MVP shall:

- Deliver a complete daily briefing
- Demonstrate end-to-end workflow execution
- Validate the platform architecture
- Produce trustworthy intelligence
- Enable daily executive use
- Establish a baseline for future enhancements

---

# Executive Value Proposition

The MVP should answer one question:

> **"What does an executive need to know in the next five minutes before beginning the workday?"**

If the MVP consistently answers this question, it is successful.

---

# Scope

The MVP focuses on essential executive awareness rather than comprehensive intelligence.

It emphasizes:

- Reliability
- Accuracy
- Consistency
- Clarity
- Actionability

---

# Core Capabilities

The MVP shall include:

## Executive Summary

A concise overview highlighting the day's most significant developments.

---

## Calendar Intelligence

Summarize:

- Today's meetings
- Scheduling conflicts
- Preparation reminders
- Travel considerations

---

## Weather Intelligence

Include:

- Current conditions
- High and low temperatures
- Sunrise
- Sunset
- Significant weather impacts

---

## News Intelligence

Summarize major national and international developments relevant to executive awareness.

---

## Cyber Intelligence

Provide:

- Critical vulnerabilities
- Active exploitation
- Major breaches
- Significant vendor announcements

Focus on actionable information rather than exhaustive reporting.

---

## Market Intelligence

Provide a concise summary of:

- Major market movements
- Economic indicators
- Technology sector highlights

---

## Executive Recommendations

Identify:

- Top priorities
- Immediate actions
- Follow-up items
- Emerging risks

Recommendations should be explicit and prioritized.

---

# Workflow

The MVP executes the following logical workflow:

```
Collect

↓

Validate

↓

Analyze

↓

Assemble

↓

Quality Review

↓

Publish
```

Every briefing should follow the same repeatable process.

---

# Report Characteristics

The MVP report should:

- Require approximately 5–7 minutes to read
- Follow a consistent structure
- Use concise language
- Clearly distinguish facts from recommendations
- Include source attribution where appropriate

---

# Out of Scope

The MVP intentionally excludes:

- Multi-user support
- Predictive analytics
- Machine learning optimization
- Complex dashboards
- Mobile applications
- Voice interfaces
- Organization-specific integrations beyond core connectors

These capabilities belong to future releases.

---

# Success Criteria

The MVP succeeds when it:

- Produces a complete briefing every day
- Requires minimal manual intervention
- Delivers accurate information
- Maintains consistent quality
- Provides clear executive value

---

# Acceptance Criteria

The MVP is considered complete when:

- Core workflows execute successfully
- Required connectors function reliably
- Reports are published automatically
- Quality assurance checks pass
- Executive recommendations are included
- Historical reports are retained

---

# Risks

Potential MVP risks include:

- Connector instability
- External API changes
- Incomplete data
- Report inconsistency
- Prompt drift

These risks should be monitored and mitigated throughout implementation.

---

# Future Expansion

The MVP establishes the platform upon which future capabilities may be added, including:

- Personalized executive profiles
- Knowledge Graph expansion
- Historical trend analysis
- Predictive intelligence
- Advanced recommendation engines
- Additional intelligence domains

Future enhancements should extend—not replace—the MVP foundation.

---

# Guiding Principle

The MVP is successful not because it includes every possible feature, but because executives choose to rely on it every morning.

Daily usefulness is the first and most important measure of success.
