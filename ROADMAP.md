# Executive Intelligence Briefing Roadmap

> Every solution should leave behind a better starting point for the next person.

---

## Purpose

The EIB Roadmap defines the planned evolution of the Executive Intelligence Briefing project.

Unlike the Manifesto, which explains *why* EIB exists, and the Constitution, which defines *how* EIB is governed, this roadmap describes *what* will be built and the order in which capabilities will mature.

The roadmap is intended to evolve as the project grows while remaining aligned with the EIB Constitution.

# Long-Term Vision

The Executive Intelligence Briefing will become a governed knowledge platform that enables individuals and organizations to preserve experience, improve decision-making, and continuously transform information into reusable intelligence.

Every enhancement should make the repository easier to understand, easier to maintain, and more valuable for future contributors.

---

## Success Criteria

The roadmap will be considered successful when:

- Governance guides every architectural decision.
- Contributors can rapidly produce consistent, high-quality artifacts.
- Knowledge becomes easier to discover over time.
- Reusable frameworks replace one-off solutions.
- Automation reduces manual effort without sacrificing quality.
- Every release leaves the repository stronger than the one before it.

## Roadmap Governance

The Roadmap is a living document.

Capabilities, priorities, and sequencing may evolve as the project matures, provided changes remain consistent with the EIB Constitution and documented architectural decisions.
